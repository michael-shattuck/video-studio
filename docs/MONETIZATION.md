# TikTok & YouTube Account Setup and Monetization Guide

## Account Separation Strategy

### Why Separate Accounts?
- Keep personal and business content distinct
- Different audience/branding for each channel
- Protect personal privacy
- Easier accounting for revenue

### What You Need
- Separate email address (use Gmail or ProtonMail)
- Separate phone number (Google Voice works for verification)
- Business name/brand identity ("Deep Dive with Rachel")

---

## TikTok Setup

### 1. Create Account
1. Download TikTok app or go to tiktok.com
2. Sign up with your separate email
3. Use Google Voice number for verification
4. Set username: `@deepdivewithrachel` or similar

### 2. Switch to Business Account
1. Go to Settings > Manage Account > Switch to Business Account
2. Select category: "Media & Entertainment" or "Education"
3. Business accounts get analytics and can add website links

### 3. Profile Optimization
- Profile photo: Use Rachel avatar or podcast logo
- Bio: "Deep dives into topics that matter. New episodes daily."
- Link: Add Linktree or YouTube channel

### 4. TikTok Monetization Requirements

**TikTok Creator Fund / Creativity Program:**
- 10,000+ followers
- 100,000+ video views in last 30 days
- 18+ years old
- Account in good standing
- Based in eligible country (US, UK, Germany, France, Italy, Spain)

**TikTok LIVE Gifts:**
- 1,000+ followers
- 18+ years old
- Can receive virtual gifts convertible to cash

**TikTok Creator Marketplace (Brand Deals):**
- 10,000+ followers recommended
- Brands reach out for sponsored content
- Rates: $200-$20,000+ per post depending on following

### 5. TikTok Content Strategy
- Post 1-3 times daily
- Best times: 7-9am, 12-3pm, 7-11pm (your audience's timezone)
- Use trending sounds when relevant
- First 1-3 seconds must hook viewer
- Engage with comments in first hour

## YouTube Setup

### 1. Create Google Account
1. Go to accounts.google.com
2. Create new account with separate email
3. Use brand name or pseudonym

### 2. Create YouTube Channel
1. Go to youtube.com, sign in
2. Click profile > Create a channel
3. Choose "Use a custom name" > "Deep Dive with Rachel"
4. This creates a Brand Account (can add managers later)

### 3. Channel Setup
- Profile picture: Rachel avatar or logo
- Banner: 2560x1440px recommended
- Description: Channel purpose, upload schedule, links
- Links: Add social media, website
- Watermark: Upload branding watermark for videos

### 4. YouTube Monetization Requirements

**YouTube Partner Program (YPP):**
- 1,000+ subscribers
- 4,000+ public watch hours in last 12 months
  - OR 10 million Shorts views in last 90 days
- Linked AdSense account
- No active Community Guidelines strikes
- 2-step verification enabled
- Follow all monetization policies

**Revenue Streams Once Monetized:**
1. **Ad Revenue**: $1-5 per 1,000 views (varies by niche)
2. **Channel Memberships**: Monthly subscriber fees ($0.99-$99)
3. **Super Chat/Stickers**: Live stream donations
4. **YouTube Premium Revenue**: Share of Premium subscriber views
5. **Merchandise Shelf**: Sell merch directly on channel

### 5. YouTube Shorts Monetization
- Separate revenue pool for Shorts
- Based on Shorts views in Shorts feed
- Lower RPM than long-form (~$0.05-0.10 per 1,000 views)
- But easier to get views

### 6. YouTube Content Strategy

**For Shorts (under 60 seconds):**
- Vertical format (1080x1920)
- Hook in first 1 second
- Add #Shorts to title or description
- Post 1-3 daily

**For Long-form:**
- 8-15 minutes ideal for ad placements
- Strong thumbnail (faces, contrast, minimal text)
- First 30 seconds critical for retention
- End screens and cards for engagement

## Cross-Platform Strategy

### Content Repurposing
1. Create long-form YouTube video (8-15 min)
2. Extract best 60-second clips for TikTok/Shorts
3. Use same audio, different visual crops
4. Stagger releases (YouTube first, then TikTok 24-48h later)

### Funnel Strategy
```
TikTok (discovery) → YouTube Shorts (transition) → YouTube Long-form (monetization)
```

## Revenue Expectations

### TikTok
- Creator Fund: ~$0.02-0.04 per 1,000 views
- 1M views = ~$20-40
- Real money is in brand deals and driving traffic elsewhere

### YouTube
- Shorts: ~$0.05-0.10 per 1,000 views
- Long-form: ~$2-5 per 1,000 views (education/news niche)
- 100K views on long-form = ~$200-500

### Timeline to Monetization
- TikTok: 1-3 months to Creator Fund eligibility with consistent posting
- YouTube: 3-6 months to YPP with consistent posting
- First $1,000/month: typically 6-12 months

## Tax & Legal Considerations

### Business Structure
- Start as sole proprietor (simplest)
- Consider LLC once revenue exceeds $50K/year
- Separate bank account for revenue

### Taxes
- Platform revenue is self-employment income
- Set aside 25-30% for taxes
- Track expenses (software, equipment, contractors)
- 1099 forms from platforms if >$600/year

### Required Accounts
1. Google AdSense (for YouTube payments)
2. PayPal or bank account (for TikTok payments)
3. Business bank account (recommended)
